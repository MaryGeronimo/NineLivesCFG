Here at Nine Lives, we strive to know all there is about cats in the media. Of course we are all familiar with The Cat in the Hat, Garfield, Hello Kitty and Grumpy Cat. But what about all the cat game characters out there?? Rover? King Tom? Palicoes? Meowth? Hopefully you recognise that last name! Cats have often been depicted in games as loveable, feisty, sometimes intimidating characters and game developers have cottoned on to just how much the public love those crazy furballs. Over the last few years, adventure games have enabled a petting function when you see a passing cat. The tamagotchi sadly lost its relevance but cat pet simulator games were born. Game developers have now created the ability to play as a cat.

Our featured cat game this month, and probably for a while, is none other than Stray. Released in 2022, Stray is an adventure game created by BlueTwelve Studio and Annapurna Interactive. The story follows a beautiful ginger stray cat (who is never named) who falls into a walled city populated by robotic beings and dangerous mutant bacteria. Playing as the cat, in 3rd person, you will set out on an adventure to return to the safety of the surface world. Along the way, you discover a drone who quickly becomes a helpful companion and friend, B-12. Whilst on your Cat Quest through the robot-inhabited world, you will mantle walls, crawl in small spaces, scratch at furniture and knock small items off shelves - if you feel like it! It is such an enjoyable experience and it is beautifully made.

The cat in Stray was initially inspired by the BlueTwelve co-founders' cat Murtaugh (pictured above). Murtaugh was a stray cat who was rescued by the producer Swann Martin-Raget and later adopted by the co-founders Viv and Koola. Murtaugh was discovered under a car when he was a kitten and was very filthy but fortunately unharmed. The producer took Murtaugh in and gave him a loving home, where he apparently spends most of the time sleeping. The studio also has two office cats that the employees would often bring in, offering developers the chance to get a quick point of reference for cat behaviours.

Another group of game cats worth mentioning are the ones found in the Animal Crossing series. The series first began in 2001 in Japan and has had 4 more game releases since. There are 30 different cat characters in the series and players can invite them to become residents on their very own island. From Ankha to Felicity to Bob, they all have their own quirks and personality traits. It's definitely worth a play if you think you would enjoy a cozy game where you can befriend cats (and a variety of other animals).
